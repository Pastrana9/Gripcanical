Introduction

Precision is something vital during the space docking process if we want a soft and accurate maneuver. In order to achieve such a goal, Gripcanical proposes a powerful and effective solution. If we split our final spaceship approach into three stages, we may identify a first rough modules axis alignment (for which we will use a vision system); a 2 final meters approach (using a pair of robotic arms); and a final fine axis alignment during the last step of the process (to provide the required precision, we will use a laser system plus an iteration algorithm). This is the way we consider space docking may reach a new level of security during mankind's future missions.

vision system

When the entering module is approaching the main module, the vision system begins to correct the position of the module, trying to align the axis of both modules. This vision system will track 3 beacons located in the main module, triangulating the position of the latch it has to connect to.

Second stage,
robotic arms and control

Once the modules are aligned and they are close enough, the robotic arms reach the entering module, grabbing it and procceeding to draw them together. This aproximation is carried out with great precission due to the feedback signals that the automatic control is receiving from the laser position system, which informs the arms how to move and correct any subtle error that the position may have.

laser system

When the two modules are close enough, the laser system activates, and it gives feedback to the arms about the relative position of both modules. This system is formed by one emitter and one reciever placed on each module. Therefore, the lasers interact and provide high precission during the final stage of the docking process.

us

Gripcanical seeks to promote and facilitate the coupling of space vehicles. This project was born from the passion of five young students from Madrid: Manuel is a development and big data student; Jaime studies Electronics and Automation Engineering; and Pablo Aerospace Engineering. Besides, we have Álvaro, a Commerce degree student; and Iván, a 3D Animation and development graduate. We try to innovate and create new methods of safe and viable spatial couplings for different environments and situations.
